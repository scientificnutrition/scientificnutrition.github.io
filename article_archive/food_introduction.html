Introduction and Motivation


Humans need vitamins and minerals in small quantities. These are called micronutrients.
Insufficient intake can lead to sickness, poor physical health, depression, and decreased life expectancy.

The FDA lists 27 micronutrients as essential for human health. It would be very difficult for an individual to keep track of their intake of each.

Rules of thumb are crude:
Adding to the average British diet an apple, cherries, an onion, cranberry juice, and chestnuts would complete the five a day, but fail to address any deficiencies.

Aiming for a colourful diet could increase variety, but there are colourful fruit (such as grapes and plums) that add little. A greener diet may be a better goal.

There is also a lot of misinformation. It may be surprising to read that:
Peppers contain more Vitamin C than oranges.
Bananas are a mediocre source of potassium.
Cheese and tahini contain equal amounts of calcium.

The aim of this project is to allow individuals to address any deficiencies in their diet with the minimum of time, effort and cost.

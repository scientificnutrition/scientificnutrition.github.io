Average British Diet


The simplest approach to addressing micronutrient deficiencies is to see what would help the average British diet
Data for most nutrients is available here. Another repository contains Iodine and Selenium data. This data is collated here. Recommended daily intakes are here.

Dividing intake by RDI and sorting it gives the table on the right hand side.

It can be seen that Vitamin D is at the top of the list. The consumption / RDI is 0.16, meaning that only 16% of the RDI is consumed in the average British diet. However, Vitamin D is produced in the skin , present in only a few foods, and can be effectively supplemented with doses between 10µg, and 15µg.

This is the first recommendation of this project: Take a 10µg Vitamin D supplement daily if it is autumn, winter, or if you have dark skin, or if your skin is rarely exposed to sunlight. (From here.)

After this, it can be seen that worst deficiencies in the average British diet are: Vitamin K, Iron, Potassium, Magnesium, Folate, Calcium, Zinc.

The next page looks at the foods that best address these deficiencies.

Best Foods to Add to the Average British diet


Data on nutrients food nutrients can be found here here, with the food groups explained in appendix B here, and is edited into a more usable file here.

From the last page, the worst deficiencies in the average British diet are: Vitamin K, Iron, Potassium, Magnesium, Folate, Calcium, Zinc.

First, each food in the database was added to the British diet in a quantity of 100g / day.
A score was calculated. Each nutrient consumption was divided by the RDI. If above 1, it was set to 1.
These were averaged, meaning that a score of 1 meant the diet was now free from any deficiencies.
Top foods were: Soya flour, tahini, sesame seeds, wheatgerm, bran, spinach, kale, almonds.
These are packed with nutrients but are also high in calories.

This was repeated by adding 100kcal / day of each food.
Top foods were: Spinach, watercress, kale, green beans, rocket, pak choi, broccoli.

For a balance between calories and weight, a combination was chosen of 50g + 50kcal / day of each food.
Top foods are on the right and have a clear theme, leading to the next result:

Your diet would almost certainly be improved by adding 100g / day leafy green vegetables.
